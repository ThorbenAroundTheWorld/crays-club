Questions serious operators ask

Get the answers you need before you commit.

Know what Crays Club Brand as a Service is, who can operate it, what you do locally, how Crays supports you, whether you need a property, what the investment can include and how fast a location can launch.

What exactly is Crays Club Brand as a Service?

It is the Crays route for approved operators to open a local club, stay or hospitality node under one global brand, governed by the Crays Association and connected to the wider Work, Live and Play ecosystem.

Do I need experience in rentals or hospitality?

Experience helps, but the stronger requirement is execution ability: asset access, local network, service mindset, financial clarity and willingness to run by Crays standards.

What is my role as franchise partner?

You build and operate the local node: property, partners, service quality, team or supplier setup, member experience and commercial discipline. Crays provides the brand system, Association governance and shared rails.

How does Crays support ongoing operations?

Through brand standards, content, booking and technology logic, partner playbooks, onboarding education, support structures, guest flow, member logic and Association feedback.

Do I already need to own a property?

No. You can apply with an owned asset, a leased asset, an investor-backed asset, a hotel or venue partnership, or a strong territory plan that can secure the right property.

How does the entry process work?

You start with a short application signal, then move into Association briefing, location review, model education, first conversation, approval and implementation planning.

What investment should I plan for?

There is no classic franchise fee, but the location still needs setup capital: property, fit-out, design, technology, operating reserve, launch content, suppliers and local service quality.

How fast can I start?

A light partner venue can move faster; a full club, hotel floor or villa conversion can take 4 to 9 months depending on property, permitting, fit-out and Association approval.
